# Context Engineering is the practice of providing relevant information, constraints, background, goals, and user-specific details to AI before asking it to perform a task. The quality of context often matters more than the prompt itself.

1 Personalized Outputs: Context allows Claude to generate recommendations tailored to your situation.
2 Higher Accuracy: Claude makes fewer assumptions when it understands the full picture.
3 Better Planning: Context enables more realistic and actionable roadmaps.
4 Real-world AI Systems: Modern AI agents rely heavily on context engineering, memory, and retrieval.

Here's your complete 30-day AI/ML Engineer roadmap — part of your ABTalks 60-Day Challenge. Click each day card to expand the full task breakdown, and switch between weeks using the tabs.
Here's how the 4 weeks are structured:
Week 1 — Foundations reforges your existing Python into ML-grade tooling. Even though you know Python, this week is about NumPy vectorization, Pandas fluency, and proper EDA methodology — the habits that separate messy notebooks from professional ones.
Week 2 — Core ML goes deep on classical algorithms with Scikit-learn, XGBoost, and MLflow. The goal isn't just "run the model" — it's understanding why each algorithm works and when to use it. You'll ship a real churn predictor with a Flask endpoint.
Week 3 — Deep Learning & LLMs formalizes your existing RAG and LLM knowledge. You'll go from neural net basics all the way to a deployed RAG chatbot on HuggingFace Spaces. The Andrej Karpathy and 3Blue1Brown resources here are must-watches — don't skip them.
Week 4 — MLOps & Capstone is what separates AI/ML engineers from data scientists. Docker, FastAPI, AWS deployment, CI/CD, and monitoring — everything needed to put a model in production. The capstone Resume Screener is designed to be a real portfolio piece.
Key resources to bookmark now: StatQuest (ML theory), Krish Naik (applied ML), TechWorld with Nana (MLOps/Docker), HuggingFace Course (free, excellent), and fast.ai (practical DL).
